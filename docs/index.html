Your agent's corner coach

Better prompts. Fewer loops. Proof it worked.

Flint is a coaching layer that lives inside your AI coding workflow. It watches how you prompt, suggests what to tighten, and builds a verification habit that sticks. Direct enough to be useful. Funny enough to keep the loop human.

What you get

One coach. Three habits.

Prompt sharpening

Flint turns "make it better" into constraints, acceptance criteria, and a clear definition of done. Your agent stops guessing.

Verification loop

Every task ends with proof. Flint nudges you to pick the smallest check that actually proves the behavior changed.

Live observation

Flint watches your session and drops tips tied to what you just did: not a wall of theory, a single next move.

Direct tasking

Ask Flint to review a prompt, tighten a spec, or suggest what to verify. It's a conversation, not a rulebook.

Codebase readiness

Run an analysis on any repo. Flint tells you what's blocking agent autonomy and gives you a prioritized fix list.

Workflow

Small loops, repeated.

1. Clarify

Goal, constraints, risks, acceptance criteria. If you can't say what "done" looks like, Flint will ask.

2. Verify

Pick the smallest checks that prove behavior. No "it probably works."

3. Ship

Lock in a regression, keep the diff reviewable, and move on.

Progress

Track the reps. See the change.

Flint tracks your sessions, tips, and streaks. Level up from Prompter to Commander. It's not gamification for its own sake; it's a mirror that shows whether your habits are actually improving.

Levels

Five ranks from Prompter to Commander. XP from sessions, tips, and streaks.

Streaks

Consecutive days build your streak. Miss a day, it resets; your best stays recorded.

Badges

Milestone, mastery, and streak badges. Earn them by doing the work.

Honest critic

You get XP for rejecting tips too. Flint respects a "no" when you can defend it.
